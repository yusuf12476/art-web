Art Bio

Imara
Wanjiku

Painter · Printmaker · Storyteller

The Story

Rooted in Soil, Raised by Colour

I grew up in the western highlands of Kakamega Forest, where mist clung to eucalyptus trees every morning and the earth smelled like rain before the rain arrived. My grandmother would grind ochre into powder and draw animals on the wall — that was my first art class.

I never meant to become a painter. I studied architecture, learned to read buildings as poems. But the canvas kept calling. After years of drafting straight lines, I needed to make something that could breathe — something that moved the way forests move, the way grief and joy happen at the same time inside you.

In 2015 I left my firm and set up a studio with two brushes and three colours. A decade later, I work across oils, acrylics, mixed media, and printmaking — all circling the same obsession: how does light decide what to love?

Art Style

Dark Lyricism & Organic Abstraction

My work lives in the tension between control and release. I build up layers — sometimes 20, sometimes 40 — and then scrape back, sand, overpaint. The final piece carries all the decisions you can't see.

Dark Naturalism
Deep tones, earthy palettes. Subjects pulled from forests, soil and memory.
Lyrical Abstraction
Forms that suggest emotion more than object — feeling always comes first.
Gestural Mark-Making
Bold brushwork — the movement of the hand visible in the final paint.
Narrative Layering
Stories buried inside paintings. Past and present on one surface.
